A white-label, end-to-end telehealth infrastructure layer for companies that want to launch or scale virtual care under their own brand, while the platform handles much of the clinical, operational, and compliance backend. Typical positioning emphasizes bringing clinical power, a nationwide provider network, and operational muscle behind the client brand.

Core platform promise
The main value proposition is speed-to-market. It lets telehealth operators avoid building a provider network, credentialing pipeline, compliance stack, and telehealth tech from scratch, so they can focus on distribution, branding, and patient acquisition. In plain terms: it is closer to “telehealth back office as a service” than a consumer-facing app.

What they provide
The offering spans several layers:
•	Clinical staffing and provider networks, including licensed clinicians and specialists.
•	Licensing and credentialing support, including multi-state coverage and NCQA-credentialed provider networks.
•	White-label technology, including private-label EHR/EMR, scheduling, booking, payments, sync and async visits, note charting, clinician management, and usage reporting.
•	ePrescribing and eLabs, plus integrations and prescription routing workflows.
•	Regulatory, legal, and compliance support for telehealth-specific operating requirements.
•	Revenue cycle management and payer coverage, including nationwide payer support and reimbursement workflows.
•	24/7 patient support and operational services that help programs run continuously.
•	AI-assisted workflow support to streamline clinical workflows and patient support.

Operational value
The platform is designed for companies that want to launch a telehealth business without assembling every component independently. Materials typically describe a “white-label backend,” “end-to-end solution,” and “plug in what you need” model, which suggests modularity: clients can buy the parts they need rather than adopting a single monolithic product. That makes it attractive for startups, digital health brands, employers, retailers, and weight-loss programs that need clinical operations more than software experimentation.
