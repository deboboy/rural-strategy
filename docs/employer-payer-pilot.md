# Pilot Program: Healthy Workplaces Eastern Washington
Mobile Cardiometabolic Screening + Vaccination Initiative

## Purpose:
Deliver free, convenient, workplace-based cardiometabolic health screenings for Eastern Washington workers, with progressive employers co-funding and hosting the program. The pilot will focus on early detection of diabetes, prediabetes, hypertension, obesity-related risk, cholesterol issues, and vaccine-preventable complications.

The program should be free to workers, easy for employers, and clinically responsible.

---

Core Model
A community clinic or mobile health provider brings a pop-up health screening site directly to participating employers’ workplaces.

Each screening event includes:

Pop-up tents or indoor tables
Clinic staff
A1C diabetes screening
Blood pressure screening
BMI / waist circumference screening
Cholesterol or lipid screening when appropriate
Cardiovascular risk assessment
Health coaching
Vaccinations, such as flu, COVID-19, Tdap, or other adult vaccines as clinically appropriate
Referrals for follow-up care
Transportation for clinic staff and supplies

Employers co-fund the program and support participation by:

Paying part of the screening cost
Hosting the event on-site
Giving workers paid time to attend
Promoting the event internally
Providing a private area for counseling and vaccination

---

Target Population
The pilot should focus on workers who face barriers to preventive care, especially in Eastern Washington communities where access may be limited by distance, cost, transportation, work schedules, or lack of a regular primary care provider.

Priority worker groups:

Food processing and agricultural workers
Warehouse, logistics, and transportation employees
Manufacturing workers
School district and public-sector employees
Small and mid-sized employers without robust benefits (1/8)
Hourly workers who cannot easily take time off for appointments
Workers with limited English proficiency
Workers with lower wages or limited insurance access

Family members may be included later, but the first pilot should focus on employees.

---

Employer Value Proposition
This program gives progressive employers a practical way to demonstrate social responsibility while improving worker health.

Employers receive:

A visible community health benefit
Improved employee retention and morale
Reduced absenteeism and presenteeism
A stronger workplace wellness offering
A way to support underserved workers
De-identified aggregate health impact reports
Positive ESG / community investment storytelling

The message to employers:

“You do not need to become a healthcare provider. You simply help bring care to the people who already trust and depend on your workplace.”

Check-in

Screening Station

A1C

Vaccination Station

Consent

Exit

After the Event
Within 3 to 7 days:

Workers with abnormal results receive follow-up contact.
High-risk workers are helped to schedule primary care, diabetes prevention, or chronic care appointments.
Employers receive a de-identified summary:
Number screened
Number vaccinated
General risk categories
Common barriers
Follow-up referral completion rate
Worker feedback

Privacy and Legal Safeguards
This is essential because workers must trust the program.

The program must ensure:

Participation is voluntary
Results are confidential
Employers receive only de-identified aggregate data
No individual health information is shared with employers
Screening is not tied to employment decisions
Workers are not pressured to disclose results
Testing follows CLIA-waived or appropriate clinical standards
Vaccines follow CDC guidance, standing orders, and cold-chain requirements
Abnormal results are handled through a clinical escalation protocol

The employer’s role is access and support, not health surveillance.

---

Clinical Follow-Up Protocol
Workers should be sorted into three categories.

Green: Normal or Low Risk
Provide preventive education
Encourage annual checkups
Offer vaccination or lifestyle resources

Yellow: Elevated Risk
Examples:

Prediabetes-range A1C
Elevated blood pressure
High BMI with metabolic risk
Borderline cholesterol

Response:

Health coaching
Diabetes prevention referral
Primary care appointment support
Nutrition and activity resources
Follow-up call within 30 days

Red: Urgent or High Risk
Examples:

Very high blood pressure
A1C in diabetes range
Symptoms suggesting urgent care need
Vaccine contraindication or adverse reaction

Response:

Immediate clinical guidance
Referral to primary care, urgent care, or emergency care when appropriate
Follow-up within 7 days
Documentation in the worker’s medical record if they consent

Pilot Narrative
This pilot turns the workplace into a point of access.
 (7/8)
Instead of asking workers to take unpaid time off, find transportation, schedule an appointment, and navigate a clinic system that may already feel distant, the program brings preventive care directly to them.

For workers, it is simple:

“Show up during your shift. Get checked. Learn your risk. Get connected if you need care.”

For employers, it is a practical act of responsibility:

“We are investing in the people who keep our business and our community moving.”

For clinics and public health partners, it creates a scalable model:

“Meet people where they are, identify risk early, and connect high-risk workers to care before small problems become emergencies.”

---

Recommended First Pilot
Start with a focused 6-month pilot:

5 employers
10 screening days
500 workers served
Basic screening for all
A1C, blood pressure, BMI, and risk counseling
Lipid panels where budget allows
Vaccinations offered at every event
Follow-up navigation for abnormal results
De-identified employer impact reports

The first pilot should prove three things:

Employers will co-fund the model.
Workers will participate when screenings are free, on-site, and paid time is provided.
The program can identify risk early and connect workers to follow-up care.

If those three are true, the model can expand across Eastern Washington through employer consortia, public health partnerships, and hospital community benefit funding. (8/8)
