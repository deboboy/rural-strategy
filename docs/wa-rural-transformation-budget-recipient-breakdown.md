# Washington Rural Health Transformation (RHT) Program — Budget & Recipient Breakdown

## Program Overview

- **Federal program:** Rural Health Transformation Program (RHTP), CFDA 93.798
- **Authorized by:** H.R. 1 (One Big Beautiful Bill Act), $50 billion nationwide over 5 years
- **Administered by:** CMS / HHS
- **Washington's Award ID:** RHTCMS332044
- **Lead state agency:** Washington State Health Care Authority (HCA), in partnership with Department of Health (DOH), Department of Social and Health Services (DSHS), and the Governor's Office
- **Performance period:** December 29, 2025 – October 30, 2030
- **Washington's total 5-year award:** $181,257,515 per year × 5 = **$906,287,575**

## How Funding Flows

Not a direct-to-hospital checkbook program. The money does NOT go directly as lump-sum payments to individual hospitals. It flows through three mechanisms:

**Direct contracts with named sub-contractors** — The state executes contracts with The Rural Collaborative, RHRC, WSHA, UW, WSU, SEIU, AAAs, RNEP, etc., who then manage the funds and distribute them to their member hospitals/clinics.

**Competitive bid / RFP process** — A large portion is distributed via RFPs posted on Washington's WEBS (Electronic Business Solution). Eligible applicants include:

- Rural hospitals and hospital districts
- Rural Health Clinics (RHCs)
- Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) — 70 rural FQHCs in WA
- Community mental health centers (CMHCs)
- EMS agencies
- Opioid treatment providers
- School districts
- Community-based organizations

**Tribal allocation** — $19M/year goes to 29 sovereign Tribal Nations. The Governor's Indian Health Advisory Council (GIHAC) decides how to divide it between Tribes. Funds flow through existing Sovereign Nation Agreements (SNAs) managed by the HCA Tribal Liaison.

## Key Recipients by Category

**Hospitals (Initiative 1):**
The Rural Collaborative (TRC) — 30 independent public hospitals receiving $5–6M/year for network development
Washington State Hospital Association (WSHA) — administers the infrastructure fund ($32–42M range/requested, adjusted within the $60M Initiative 1 total) for hospital IT, cybersecurity, revenue cycle tech
All 39 Critical Access Hospitals (CAHs) and 3 Sole Community Hospitals are eligible beneficiaries under the infrastructure fund and incentive programs
13–17 of WA's 39 CAHs are currently in financial distress per Flex Monitoring indicators

**Community & Social Services (Initiative 2):**
Area Agencies on Aging (AAAs) — dementia capable communities ($3–4M)
SEIU Training Benefit Group — embedding LTCW testing at training sites
WA 211 — call center expansion for triage
Mobile Care-A-Van and AAA Mobile Assistance Van — mobile health services

**Tribal Nations (Initiative 3):**
29 federally recognized Tribes in Washington
Allocation decided by GIHAC; flows through Sovereign Nation Agreements

**Technology (Initiative 4):**
University of Washington — Project ECHO ($4M)
Rural hospitals, FQHCs, RHCs — via provider technology fund RFP ($15M)
Spheregen, Oracle, Microsoft — referenced as vendors for AI/cybersecurity tooling supporting the infrastructure fund

**Workforce (Initiative 5):**
University of Washington WWAMI — Rural Family Medicine Residency ($5–6M)
Washington State University (WSU) — rural health education programs ($2.3M)
Rural Nursing Education Program (RNEP) — place-based nursing education ($3–4M)
SEIU Training Benefit Group — grow-your-own training
Individual rural health professionals — incentive bonuses ($9–10M pool, ~50–100 recipients/year 1)

**Behavioral Health (Initiative 6):**
EMS and Opioid Treatment Providers (identified by HCA) — mobile crisis ($10–11M) and OTP incentives ($6–7M)
Behavioral health clinics — CCBHC transition support ($3–4M)
School districts — school-based behavioral health services ($5–6M)

**State Agencies (Administration):**
HCA (lead), DOH, DSHS — $4M for ~37 FTEs and program management

## Important Constraints

- Cannot be used for: construction/building expansion, clinician salary supports with non-compete clauses, covered telecom/surveillance equipment, independent R&D, meals, lobbying, or supplanting existing funding
- Provider payments capped at 15% of total award
- Renovations capped at 20% of total award
- Administrative overhead capped at 10% (most activities limited to 7.1%)
- Rural Tech Catalyst Fund — no more than 10% for new tech development
- EMR replacement — no more than 5%
